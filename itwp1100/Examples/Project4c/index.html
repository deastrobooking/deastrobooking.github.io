My favorite season in Michigan is Garden season

Of course, there isn't an official "Garden" season in Michigan. If it were a season, it might start in May (March for some that start seeds indoors) and end when your last plant expires. Each year, I get different results and length of harvest time depending on the weather. Usually, before the first frost (generally sometime in late September), I will harvest every tomato that has the slightest bit of color. The frost won't necessarily kill the plants, but the cold generally zaps the flavor of the fruit -- but that's a different website. See Harvest Time For Tomatoes

I suppose I should include the following disclaimer: I am not now, nor have I ever been, a professional gardener.

My goal is to share a few things that I've learned about growing a few different types of vegetables over the past decade or two. I consider myself to be a hobbyist gardener -- I don't get too intense about it. For me, gardening is peaceful work with delicious return on investment -- there usually is quite an investment.
